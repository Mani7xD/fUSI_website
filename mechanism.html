How Functional Ultrasound Imaging Works

The core mechanism behind functional ultrasound imaging involves ultrafast Doppler ultrasound techniques. Unlike conventional ultrasound that operates at a few dozen frames per second, fUSI systems capture thousands of frames per second. This high frame rate is key to detecting subtle changes in blood flow through tiny vessels in the brain.

These high-speed acquisitions allow the formation of Power Doppler images, which represent cerebral blood volume. By observing temporal fluctuations in these images, neural activity can be inferred indirectly. When a brain region becomes active, the corresponding increase in blood flow is detected and mapped with impressive accuracy.

Ultrasound pulses are transmitted and the returning echoes from red blood cells are captured. Signal processing algorithms then separate slow-moving tissue signals from the faster-flowing blood, allowing detailed imaging of vascular dynamics at the capillary level. This makes fUSI ideal for capturing both transient and long-term brain activity patterns.
