Breakthroughs and Innovations

Over the past decade, fUSI has rapidly evolved from a preclinical technique to one with increasing potential for human application. Innovations in both hardware and computational analysis have expanded its usability and accuracy.

3D Functional Imaging: With the advent of matrix-array probes and high-throughput systems, volumetric brain imaging is now possible, enabling researchers to study entire brain regions in real-time rather than single cross-sections.

Wearable fUSI Devices: Miniaturization has led to the development of head-mounted and portable fUSI systems suitable for small animals and potentially ambulatory human applications.

AI Integration: Deep learning algorithms are now used to denoise, reconstruct, and interpret fUSI datasets faster and more accurately than manual approaches, opening new possibilities for clinical diagnostics and real-time feedback.
