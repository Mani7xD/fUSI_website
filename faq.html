What makes fUSI different from fMRI?

fUSI offers superior spatial and temporal resolution at a fraction of the cost and size. It is portable, silent, and more sensitive to microvascular changes than fMRI.

Can fUSI be used in humans?

Yes. While initially used in animals, miniaturized and wearable fUSI probes have enabled its application in neonates, during surgeries, and in ICU environments.

Is fUSI invasive or safe?

fUSI is non-invasive and uses standard ultrasound frequencies that are already deemed safe for diagnostic imaging. It does not require ionizing radiation or contrast agents.

What are the limitations of fUSI?

It is less effective in adults with thick skulls unless invasive windows are used. Motion artifacts and limited depth penetration can also affect image quality.
