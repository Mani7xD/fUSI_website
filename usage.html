Where fUSI is Used Today

Functional Ultrasound Imaging has transitioned from lab-based studies to real-world settings. Institutions like Caltech, INSERM (France), and the University of Zurich are pioneers in utilizing fUSI for cutting-edge neuroimaging research.

It is primarily employed in preclinical research, particularly in rodents, due to its ability to record real-time brain activity with minimal invasiveness. These studies explore cognition, pharmacological testing, and disease modeling (e.g., Parkinsonâ€™s, epilepsy).

In clinical contexts, fUSI is gaining traction for neonatal brain imaging, where its silent, radiation-free operation offers a safe alternative to MRI. Its portability and speed also make it a strong candidate for bedside neuromonitoring in intensive care units and intraoperative settings.
