Foundational Principle

Functional Ultrasound Imaging (fUSI) is rooted in the concept of neurovascular coupling — the physiological process where increased neuronal activity leads to localized increases in cerebral blood flow. As neurons activate, they consume more oxygen and nutrients, prompting nearby blood vessels to dilate and increase perfusion. This vascular response is a proxy for neural activity.

fUSI captures these hemodynamic changes using ultrafast Doppler techniques. By acquiring ultrasound data at several thousand frames per second, it allows researchers to visualize blood volume fluctuations in tiny capillaries and venules. This rapid acquisition is what enables fUSI to provide real-time imaging of brain activity with excellent temporal and spatial resolution.

The data acquired through fUSI is processed to create a dynamic map of brain function — essentially a movie of the brain’s hemodynamic responses. This is particularly valuable for mapping spontaneous neural events, evoked responses, and functional connectivity between brain regions without the need for contrast agents or invasive probes.
